Netsuke Sculptures


A netsuke is a small sculptural object which has gradually developed in Japan over a period of more than three hundred years. The traditional form of Japanese dress, the kimono, had no pockets. Thus a small purse, or sagemono was hung from the wearers obi or sash. The netsuke slipped under the sash and prevented the sagemono from slipping out from under the sash and falling to the ground.
